# Tips & Best Practices

## Getting Started

- Start from a preset and modify — it's faster than starting from scratch
- Use **Save Design** frequently — there's no auto-save
- The **Undo** button (or ⌘Z) is your friend — experiment freely

## Shapes

- **SuperFormula** is the most versatile shape — spend time with `m` and `n1` before touching the others
- **Shape Morphing** (Bottom → Top Shape) creates dramatic transformations that are hard to achieve any other way
- Shapes with thin lobes (Spirograph, Butterfly) look great in renders but need thick walls to print

## Textures

- The **master Textures toggle** is a global on/off — set up individual textures with it off, then flip it on
- **Textures stack additively** — combining 2–3 textures creates richness that a single texture can't match
- Great combos: Fluting + Simplex, Rods + Vertical Rods, Basket Weave + Wave Twist
- **SVG Pattern** is the most powerful texture — try [PatternMaker](https://patternmaker.dcity.org) for seamless SVG patterns that were designed specifically for VaseMaker

## Profile & Form

- The **Profile Curve** editor has more impact on the final look than almost anything else — a great profile makes a simple shape look elegant
- **Custom Twist** creates dramatic spiral effects — a quarter to half turn is usually enough
- **Wave Twist + Fluting** is a killer combination

## Printing

- Use **Show Facets** before exporting to check if resolution is sufficient
- Enable **Smooth Inner** when using deep textures to prevent thin walls
- For display pieces: vase mode (wall thickness = 0) + high radial resolution looks stunning

## Image Capture

- The capture frame shows exactly what will be saved — compose carefully
- Use custom dimensions (2048×2048 or larger) for high-quality portfolio shots
- Rulers are hidden in captures automatically
